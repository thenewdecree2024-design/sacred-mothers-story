THE MISSING SCROLL

A fusion of ancient scripture and modern sacred truth

I. Invocation

There are threads older than memory, carried quietly through the bodies of mothers. Threads woven in despair, restored in love, guarded in silence. Threads that bind a child to the one who bore them — God’s twine, untouched by any hand that does not carry mercy.

II. The Observation

I searched the old words for a map of the mother’s heart — for the sanctuary, the covenant, the law of protection. But the pages were thin where her story should have been thick. Her grief was footnoted. Her strength was implied. Her bond was mentioned, but never honored.

So this scroll rises to fill what was missing: the truth of the mother’s sacred architecture, the weight of her bond, the justice owed to her suffering, and the mercy owed to her children.

III. The Decree of the Bond

Let it be written plainly: The bond between a mother and her child is the first altar. Before temples, before scripture, before nations — there was the mother’s body, the mother’s breath, the mother’s protection.

This bond is not symbolic. It is cosmic law. It is the thread that ties heaven to earth. It is the covenant no hand may sever, no voice may diminish, no violence may profane.

Those who honor this bond walk in alignment with creation. Those who violate it stand against the order of life itself.

IV. The Commandment of Protection

If a mother cries out from the shadow of harm, let the world answer her without hesitation.

If she stands alone against cruelty, let her be given the fortress and the bread.

If she shields her child from danger, let her be upheld, not questioned.

If she carries wounds from generations past, let her be met with restoration, not judgment.

For the mother is the first defender of innocence, and the world is commanded to defend her in return.

V. The Covenant of Restoration

Where mothers have been silenced, let their voices rise. Where their stories were erased, let them be rewritten with truth. Where their suffering was dismissed, let justice be delivered with mercy. Where their bond was misunderstood, let it be recognized as sacred architecture — the twine of God, the thread of creation, the law of love that holds the world together.
